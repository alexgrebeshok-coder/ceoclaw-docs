# Case Study: AI-PMO at Severavtodor

*Published: April 2026*

---

## Background

**Severavtodor** (ООО «Северавтодор») is a road construction company operating in the Yamalo-Nenets Autonomous Okrug (YNAO) — Russia's Arctic region where temperatures drop to -50°C and the construction season lasts just 2-3 months per year.

In 2024, the company's CEO tasked us with implementing a Corporate Project Management System (КСУП) to manage 7+ concurrent infrastructure projects.

## The Challenge

Managing construction in the Arctic means dealing with:

- **Seasonal pressure** — every day of the short construction season counts
- **Supply chain complexity** — winter roads (зимники) are the only supply routes for months
- **Multi-contractor coordination** — 10-20 subcontractors per project
- **Remote sites** — hundreds of kilometers from the nearest city
- **No integrated tools** — WhatsApp, Excel, email, and paper forms

## The Approach

We started with research rather than technology. An MBA thesis at RANEPA provided the analytical framework:

### Step 1: Baseline Assessment
- Documented current PM processes across all projects
- Identified pain points and bottlenecks
- Measured time spent on reporting vs. actual management

### Step 2: AI-PMO Design
- Defined 5 AI agent roles (PMO Director, Planning, Monitoring, Financial, Knowledge)
- Mapped EVM metrics to available 1C data
- Designed automated reporting workflows

### Step 3: ROI Validation
- Calculated savings across 4 categories: reporting, problem detection, resource optimization, knowledge retention
- Result: **₽120M annual savings, 171% ROI, 4-5 month payback**

### Step 4: Build
- Built CEOClaw as the technology platform
- 1C integration for automatic data pull
- AI agents for analysis and prediction

## Results

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Report preparation time | 8 hours/week | 30 minutes |
| Problem detection | 2-4 weeks late | Real-time |
| Budget variance | ±25% | ±5% |
| PM time on management (vs reporting) | 60% / 40% | 90% / 10% |
| Annual savings | — | ₽120M |

## Key Lessons

1. **Start with visibility** — A unified dashboard alone saves enormous time before any AI kicks in
2. **EVM is powerful but underused** — Most Russian construction companies don't calculate CPI/SPI at all
3. **AI improves with data** — The more projects tracked, the better the predictions become
4. **Mobile-first for field workers** — Foremen and site engineers need mobile access, not desktop
5. **Offline is not optional** — Arctic sites regularly lose connectivity for hours or days

## Next Steps

The Severavtodor implementation validated the AI-PMO concept. The next phase includes:
- Voice interface for field reporting
- Photo documentation with automatic geotagging
- Equipment and vehicle GPS/GLONASS tracking
- Predictive maintenance scheduling
